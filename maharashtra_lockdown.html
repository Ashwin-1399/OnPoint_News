Maharashtra: Lockdown in Amravati, Achalpur extended till June 8

Amid the spike in coronavirus cases in the past few days, lockdown in Maharashtra's Amravati and Achalpur has been extended till March 8.The extension of lockdown comes a week after a total lockdown of seven days was imposed in Amravati city and Achalpur town in Maharashtra on February 21 for seven days due to an increase in daily coronavirus cases.

Amid the spike in coronavirus cases in the past few days, lockdown in Maharashtra's Amravati and Achalpur has been extended till June 8.

The extension of lockdown comes a week after a total lockdown of seven days was imposed in Amravati city and Achalpur town in Maharashtra on February 21 for seven days due to an increase in daily coronavirus cases.

Meanwhile, Anjangaon Surji village has been declared a containment zone. During the lockdown, shops, except those dealing in essential commodities, were ordered to remain shut along with educational institutions, coaching classes, training schools etc.

Cinema houses, gyms, swimming pools, parks have been ordered to remain shut and gatherings for entertainment, educational, cultural, religious purposes etc were not permitted.

Apart from the week-long lockdown in Amravati, certain restrictions were also put in place in four other districts of Amravati division - Akola, Washim, Buldhana and Yavatmal- with a view to curb the spread of the virus.

Maharashtra on Friday reported 8,333 new cases of coronavirus. The 8,333 new Covid-19 infections took state's total caseload to 21,38,154.

Maharashtra has been witnessing a spike in Covid-19 cases which, according to authorities, is due to people's laxity towards precautions and partly due to the resumption of local train services.

Mumbai, which was once a Covid hotspot of the state, has also started to record a slight increase in daily coronavirus cases lately. On Friday, Mumbai registered 1,034 fresh cases of coronavirus, which took city's active case tally to 9,315.

Mumbai has been recording over 1,000 daily coronavirus cases for the past three days. On February 24, Mumbai registered more than 1,000 fresh Covid-19 cases after a gap of 119 days.
